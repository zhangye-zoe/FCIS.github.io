Abstract

Cell instance segmentation is fundamental to analyzing biomedical images, yet accurately distinguishing tightly touching cells remains a persistent challenge. We propose a novel framework inspired by the four-color theorem to guarantee adjacent cells receive distinct labels. Our asymptotic training strategy, encoding transformation, and negative sampling yield state-of-the-art performance on multiple datasets.

Overview

By treating cells as ‚Äúcountries‚Äù and background as ‚Äúocean,‚Äù our four-color encoding reformulates instance segmentation into a four-class semantic task, simplifying post-processing and improving efficiency.

Method

Asymptotic Training: Separate foreground segmentation before four-color labeling.

Encoding Transformation: Learnable module to achieve minimal-color consistency.

Negative Sampling: Orthogonality constraints enforce distinct adjacent labels.
